HOW TO USE EITHER OR NEITHER IN ENGLISH GRAMMAR:

What is the difference between neither and either, and how they are used.

Answer:
  1. Either and neither's meaning change in accordance with the context of a sentence.
  2. Either is generally considered as positive and neither is opposite (negative).
  3. They are generally used when we talk about one or two persons, things etc.. [PLEASE NOTE THAT: THEY ARE NOT USED WHEN WE TALK ABOUT MORE THAN TWO PERSONS OR THINGS.]
  4. Either and neither can be used as:
    1. Adjective
    2. Pronoun
    3. Conjunction
    4. Adverb

When used as an Adjective either means "one or the other of two people or things," and neither means "not one or othes of two people or things." In other words, we can say that neither means "not either". The following example sentence show its use:

  • You may borrow either book. [You may borrow one or the other of two books]
  • You may borrow neither book. [You may not borrow one or the other of two books, you may not borrow either book.]

When used an a pronoun either means "one or the other." Neither means "not the one and not the other" or "not either". The following example sentence show its usage:

  • You may wear either dresses to the party. [You may wear one dress or you may wear the other dress]
  • You may wear neithers of the dresses to the party. [You may not wear the one dress or you may not wear the other dresses; you may not wear the either dress]

When used as conjunction either is used with "or" to indicate choices and possibilities. Neither is used with "nor" to indicate two or more people, things and actions etc. about which something is not true. The following sentence example show its use:

  • I will eat either chicken or fish tonight. [I will choose one of two options]
  • I wll eat neither chicken or fish tonight. [I will not eat chicken and I will not eat fish; I will not choose either of these two foods]
